### Broken Relationships: De-Risking by Correspondent Banks and International Trade

*With Lea Borchert, Ralph de Haas, and Karolin Kirschenmann.*
**Forthcoming, *Review of Financial Studies*.**

We examine how the termination of correspondent banking relationships affects international trade, using export data from emerging Europe. When local banks lose correspondent services, small and medium-sized exporters experience significant declines in trade, and some firms cease operations entirely.

[SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4658618) · [RFS replication package](https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/rfs) · [Replication code](https://github.com/alisonschultz/BrokenRelationships)

### Guns and Kidneys: How Transplant Tourism Finances Global Conflict

**Revise and Resubmit, *Journal of the European Economic Association*.** Awarded the Young Economist Award.

Using georeferenced conflict data and records of transplant infrastructure, I investigate how organ trafficking shapes local conflict. A one-standard-deviation increase in the US waiting list for kidneys raises conflict probability by 17%.

[SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4045584)

### Banks of a Feather: The Informational Advantages of Being Alike

*With Peter Bednarek, Valeriya Dinger, and Natalja von Westernhagen.*
**Published in *Journal of Money, Credit, and Banking*.**

Using German credit register data, we show that banks lend more to similar banks: portfolio similarity helps overcome information asymmetries in interbank markets.

[SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4536999)

### The Precious Networks of the Rich: How the Wealthiest Shape the Tax Agenda

*With Martí Medina Hernandez, Benjamin Rosche, and Yufang Sun.*
**Scheduled for the IIPF Annual Meeting 2026.**

We test whether social and institutional ties between legislators and the ultra-rich help explain trends toward regressive taxation. We use large language models to classify the progressivity of tax bills and construct a dataset of billionaire–congressional ties based on educational overlaps.

### Profit Shifting via Carbon Emission Trading: First Indications

**Presented at VfS and IIPF 2024; scheduled for the World Inequality Conference 2026.**

I analyse the EU Emission Trading Scheme for signs of exploitation by multinationals, documenting abnormally high internal allowance trades at year-end within the same ownership structures — a pattern consistent with regulatory and tax arbitrage.
